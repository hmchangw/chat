# Software / IT Salary Comparison — Poland vs. USA

> **Scope & caveats.** Figures are **approximate gross annual compensation in USD** for
> software and IT roles, reflecting 2025–2026 market conditions. They are illustrative
> estimates compiled from general market knowledge — **not** scraped from a live salary
> database — and will vary widely by city, company tier (startup vs. FAANG/big tech),
> contract type, and individual negotiation.
>
> - **Poland** numbers reflect major tech hubs (Warsaw, Kraków, Wrocław, Gdańsk) and
>   blend permanent-employment (UoP) and B2B contract rates, converted at ~4.0 PLN/USD.
> - **USA** numbers are national figures; high-cost hubs (SF Bay Area, NYC, Seattle)
>   trend toward the upper end, while lower-cost regions trend toward the lower end.
> - Ranges are **base + typical cash**; they generally **exclude equity/stock**, which can
>   be a large additional component in US big tech.

## Key takeaways

- **The gap is largest at the junior level (~4x)** and narrows somewhat with seniority,
  though US compensation remains roughly **2.5x–3.5x** higher across the board in nominal USD.
- **Equity changes the picture at the top end.** US senior/staff roles at well-funded
  companies often add $50k–$300k+ in annual stock vesting, widening the real gap beyond
  what the base-cash table shows.
- **Cost of living offsets part of the difference.** Poland's living costs are materially
  lower than US tech hubs, so purchasing-power-adjusted gaps are smaller than the nominal
  USD ratios suggest — though still favorable to the US for savings potential.
- **Contract type matters in Poland.** B2B (self-employed contractor) rates typically sit
  10–25% above equivalent permanent-employment gross figures but shift tax/benefit burden
  to the worker; the ranges above blend both.
